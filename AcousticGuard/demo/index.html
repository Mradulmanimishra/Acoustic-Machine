🧠 How It Works

A Convolutional Autoencoder trains exclusively on healthy machine sounds. At inference, it tries to reconstruct new audio as a spectrogram. A healthy sound reconstructs well (low MSE). A faulty sound reconstructs poorly — that error IS the fault signal.

📡 No Sensors Required

Unlike vibration-based systems that require mounting accelerometers on every machine, AcousticGuard uses standard microphones placed nearby. Setup takes minutes, not days. Scales to hundreds of machines at a fraction of traditional PdM cost.

🚀 Production Ready

Edge deployment on CPU in <50ms latency. 600KB model footprint. Runs on Raspberry Pi. Supports live streaming, batch processing, REST API, and webhook alerts. Ships with a Docker container and Makefile.
